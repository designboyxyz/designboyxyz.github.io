the product

politimap is a reponsive web app that makes it easier for voters to access political information.

when users input their location, the app scrapes relevant information from government authorized websites and presents it to them.

project duration

november 2021
to december 2021



background...

the problem

it's hard to keep track of political information because there's too much to process, it's all over the place, and overwhelming

the goal

design an app that presents political information to users in way that is accessible

my role

ux designer, designing the app from conception to delivery

responsibilities

conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs


understanding the user...



user research summary...

i conducted interview and created empathy maps to understand the users i’m designing for and their needs. a primary user group identified through research was working adults who don’t have time to research political information on their own.

this user group confirmed assumptions about politimap users, but research also revealed that time was not the only factor limiting users. other user problems included poor knowledge of political processess and inability to locate key political information.


understanding user pain points...


time

working adults are too busy to do their own political research

accessibility

political information is buried behind several webpages and hard to access

usability

government freedom of information websites don't offer the best user experience.

accuracy

voters want access to information that they know is reliable, and not manipulated by private interests

starting the design...



paper wireframes...

i decided that i wanted the app to be presented on one page, with a navigtion bar that allowed the user to jump to any part of the site.

each part has its own functionalities. for instance, the section titled 'legal codes' opens a popup whenever the user clicks a code category from the list. when users close out of this popup, they return back to the section of the page they were on.

each section relates to a specific part of the dataset. for instance, the section titled 'map of territory' depicts geographic data about region boundaries on a map.


digital wireframes...


as the initial design phase continue, I made sure to base screens on feedback and findings that I was getting from the user research.
it was important to make screens based on the data. every part of our dataset had to be visually represented on the screen in the best way possible.

usability study findings...


affinity diagram from one of the usability studies

round 1 findings

  • representative profiles should be detailed
  • legal codes should be easy to read
  • sorting information by the city,county, state, and national level helps organize our dataset and display information

round 2 findings

  • users want to see quick statistics about representatives and the territories they occupy
  • language and accessibility features are important for users who not see, or read english

accessibility considerations...


i provided access to users who are vision impaired through adding alt text to images for screen readers.

by enabling readibility in different languages and text-to-speech audio in popular languages, we open the app to more voters.


going forward...



takeaways...


impact

voters think that the app is useful and is going to help them become more civically engaged.

“i didn't know all this stuff was going on! i'm excited to keep up with it all.”
—feedback from a user

what i learned

while designing politimap, i learned that accessibility is important in designing an app that is going to be used about just about everyone. learning from users allowed me to discover new journey possibilities i had never thought of!


next steps...


pain point testing

conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points of the users have been addressed.

expanding dataset

we expand our dataset based on what information voters want to know about

enabling discourse

politimap does not enable discussions, it just shows the information. it would be interesting to walk through a feature where users can tweet out information, or comment their thoughts right on the page.
