red tiger tea is a tea brand that wants to create a better tea experience by providing beautiful design, wholesome ingredients, and great taste.

my role

artistic direction, user analysis, ingredient selection, and prototyping


results

a scalable tea-brand that stands out on shelves and is creates memorable and shareable experiences with drinkers.



a better tea experience...

we wanted to create a better craft blended tea experience, and we centered that experience around: the design of the box and bags, the blends, and the accessibility.


red tiger tea has an eye-catching box design and logo, blends that taste great and provide many health benefits, with availibility through major retailers online and nationwide.



design priorities...

eye-catching aesthetic

the goal is to have a box that stands out, communicates the tea blend, has a logo that is easy to recognize, and looks nice placed in home spaces.

health overview

the back of each box shares the health benefits of each ingredient.

scalable design

if we want to add new blends, we want a design that's going to let us be able to do that.

communicating facts

we want to communicate all important food-related information using familiar icons.


how it came to be...

an early prototype


we started by identifying what blends we were going to sell, then we prototyped the box art, and virtually shelf-tested the design in focus groups to see whether our product caught the eye of the average consumer, and later designed media for marketing materials.


mapping the information onto the box template


what we're learning...

people want boxes that look good

people reported that boxes with colorful art stood out to them. they appreciated interesting designs.

details on health benefits are useful

knowing exactly how ingredients benefit health really gets people on board—especially if ingredients work together in unique ways.


creating memorable experiences...

each one of our tea bags has a random emoji (from a selection of emojis we've approved). quotes on tags are overdone, and emojis are filled with meaning. the brand also stands out because it's the only brand that does this.
